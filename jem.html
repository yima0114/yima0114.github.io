Jewelry

Yi Ma’s jewelry and material practice explores how form, surface, structure, weight, and proximity change when an object is worn, held, or placed near the body. The works include metal jewelry and wearable sculpture, alongside experiments in paper, felt, foam, and acrylic. Some works extend her pipe-focused practice; others develop independently through bodily observation, humor, symbolism, and material experimentation.

Featured Series

Nipple Brooch Series Metal brooches reinterpret soft, intimate bodily forms through rigid surfaces, scale, and fastening, bringing recognition, humor, and bodily attention into wearable form.

Placenta Sculpture

Paper Placenta is a collaborative image-based project with Harry Williams. Yi Ma designed and made the kozo-paper sculpture and appears in the work as the model. Its lightness, translucency, and fibrous structure suggest protection, attachment, and biological connection.
