Research framework

Research / Archive

A painting-led inquiry into pipe-based public infrastructures, material translation, wearable/body translation, exhibition as research site, public recognition, and contextual archive.

Painting-led inquiry

The work begins with site observation and painting. Fire hydrants, pipes, valves, maintenance devices, and related public infrastructures are treated as familiar forms that remain visible in everyday space but are often passed over without attention.

Painting isolates these forms from their functional surroundings. The image becomes a controlled perceptual situation: recognizable enough to hold public memory, displaced enough to make the object newly visible.

Translation methods

  • Site observation of hydrants, pipes, valves, and maintenance devices
  • Painting as perceptual access and visual reconfiguration
  • Screenprint, proofing, cropping, and paper-based image transfer
  • Wearable translation through bodily proximity, scale, and placement
  • Metal, resin, and constructed form as tactile material translation
  • Exhibition, walking, and shared recognition as public research methods

Contextual archive

The archive will gather field notes, visual references, public responses, contributed pipe photographs, and site-memory materials connected to fire infrastructure and civic maintenance systems.

Jewelry, metalwork, 3D forms, wearable works, and material studies are included only as extensions of this same research logic: material translation, bodily proximity, perceptual displacement, and public recognition.

Archive materials and documentation are forthcoming.
