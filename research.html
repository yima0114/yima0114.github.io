Research framework

Research / Archive

This archive gathers a practice that moves through translation: from text to image, from painting to material, from object to body, and from body to public encounter. It traces how ordinary forms become visible through shifts in attention, surface, scale, and recognition.

Painting-led inquiry

The work begins with observation and painting. Pipes, hydrants, valves, and other ordinary urban forms appear in daily life, but they often remain outside focused attention. Painting slows this encounter and allows form, surface, color, and scale to become newly visible.

Through translation, the image moves into other materials. Screenprint, metal, paper, resin, and wearable forms transform the painted image into objects that can be touched, worn, carried, or encountered in space.

Translation methods

  • Site observation of hydrants, pipes, valves, and maintenance forms
  • Painting as a method of attention, distance, and recognition
  • Screenprint, cropping, proofing, and paper-based image transfer
  • Wearable translation through bodily scale, proximity, and placement
  • Metal, resin, paper, and constructed form as tactile translation
  • Exhibition, walking, conversation, and public response as research encounters

Contextual archive

The archive brings together visual references, field notes, material experiments, exhibition traces, and public responses. These materials do not explain the work from outside; they show how the practice develops through attention, transformation, and encounter.
