Yi Ma

Artist-Researcher | Painting-Led & Material Artistic Research

Literature → Oil Painting → Jewelry / Material Translation → Public Infrastructure Research

Yi Ma works across realist oil painting, jewelry, wearable objects and artistic research. Trained in literature, oil painting and jewelry design, her practice investigates how overlooked urban infrastructure can become visible, recognizable and discussable through painting-led inquiry, material translation and exhibition-based research.

02

Artistic Research Practice

Painting-Led Observation

Fire Hydrant / Pipe Series
2021–present

Painting-led investigation of overlooked urban infrastructure, focusing on how scale, colour, surface, maintenance traces and architectural surroundings shift everyday objects from background visibility into perceptual focus.

Material / Wearable Translation

Pipe Wearable Pieces / Pipe Epaulettes
2023–present

Material translations that move pipe-based infrastructure from distant urban background into bodily proximity. These works transform functional structures, architectural textures, decorative languages and environmental traces into wearable forms.

Public-Facing Documentation

Pipe Lady / This Is Not a Pipe
2021–present

A developing artistic research project using exhibition, audience feedback, collaborator meetings and site-based documentation to test how pipe-based infrastructure becomes visible, recognizable and discussable.
