Yi Ma

Artist | Painting-Led Practice, Material Translation, and Public Encounter

Literature and Close Reading | Painting and Site Observation | Material and Wearable Translation | Exhibition and Public Encounter

Yi Ma works across realist oil painting, jewelry, wearable sculpture, and material experimentation. Painting is the visual foundation of her practice, while her background in literature informs close attention to context, language, and meaning. Across different projects, selected images and forms are reworked through scale, material, touch, and bodily relation. Exhibitions, conversations, and audience responses can open new directions for later work.

02

Artistic Research Practice

Painting-Led Observation

Fire Hydrant / Pipe Series
2021–present

The Fire Hydrant / Pipe Series is an ongoing body of realist oil paintings developed through repeated observation in San Francisco. Pipes and hydrants become focal subjects within selected street fragments, carrying the light, color, surface, repair, and architectural character of their surroundings. As a group, the paintings place pipe-based infrastructure between still life and cityscape, shaped by place and time.

Material / Wearable Translation

Pipe-Derived Material and Wearable Practice
2023–present

The Pipe Shoulder Piece and pipe-form accessories extend the pipe-focused practice at bodily scale. Jewelry processes, 3D-printed models, casting, and metalwork reshape recurring pipe forms through structure, weight, touch, and placement on the body. Architectural, historical, and situated references inform specific material decisions.

Public Encounter

Pipe Lady: Solo Exhibition
2022

Pipe Lady brought pipe-focused oil paintings together with same-size translations printed on silk scarves. The Mean Comments Only guestbook invited detailed viewer responses beyond polite approval. A city engineer later used a scarf as a marker during an experimental guided walk linking pipes to San Francisco’s earthquake and fire history. These encounters formed an early basis for using exhibition and public responses in subsequent artistic decisions.
