Pipe Lady

Pipe Lady begins with ordinary pipe-based forms that appear throughout the city but often remain at the edge of attention. Through repeated painting, image translation, wearable objects, and exhibition, the project follows how these forms shift from background structures into images, materials, and encounters shaped by perception, recognition, memory, and place.

Oil Painting Repeated painting slows attention, turning pipe forms into images of distance, surface, and recognition. Printmaking Screenprint and paper transfer transform the painted image through repetition, cropping, pressure, and surface. Pipe Wearable Piece Pipe-derived forms move from urban space to bodily scale, becoming objects of touch, weight, and encounter.

Placenta Sculpture

A paper-based wearable sculpture made with kozo paper. The work translates the organic form of the placenta into a fragile body-related object, using paper’s lightness, translucency, and fibrous structure to suggest protection, attachment, and biological connection.
